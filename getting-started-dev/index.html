Introduction to the Design System

Our design system is a product, not a project. We will iterate often and collaborate continually, ensuring our content meets the diverse business needs of Tyler. It will be a consolidation of UX patterns, resources, components, processes and guidance, which enables visual and interactive consistency and creates efficiency through shared, re-usable components. Contributions to our design system will be welcomed from all product teams and will be curated by the Forge Advisory Board. Our design system captures what we have accomplished, and relies on the product teams for future growth – through their ideation, exploration and contribution.


Components:

Individual pieces of an interface that combine together to display data and add value. Examples: cards, data tables, alerts

Patterns:

Methods of designing interactions that combine components in a meaningful way. Examples: editing, bulk adding, saving, onboarding

Page Layouts:

Groups of patterns and components that create your app structure. Examples: laying out search pages or faceted results, detail pages

Adoption

The value of Forge is realized when products ship features using parts from the system. The more the design system is used, the faster it will evolve with best practices and community contributions.
