Data Viz: Colors

Color is a key component to data visualization. Ensure your colors communicate what they should and are ADA compliant as appropriate with the following palettes.  

Single hue palettes

You will use these palettes most of the time.
For situations where your data is not categorically different, and/or the chart should have less visual emphasis. These palettes emphasize a difference in hue and brightness to maximize the experience for visually impaired users as well as our non-visually impaired users.

Pink/Red (Highest Emphasis)

I would encourage use of this palette in rare circumstances: when you are calling direct attention to multiple pieces of data that needs to be attended to. The use of red indicates “emergency” and “warning” so use it wisely. This can also cause more issues with colorblindness for protanopia.

Additional palettes

If the above (“colorblind friendly”) palette does not meet your needs, you may use the following “basic” palette (8 colors), and it’s “extended version” (12 colors) as well.

However, if you use this palette note that you MUST label your data so that it is apparent what the takeaway from the chart is. We recommend this with ALL charts, but it is particularly important with these colors. You will see this reflected in these examples via the percentages at the top of each bar.

Extended palette

Start by using the basic palette, and then if you need additional colors, you may use this extended version.
The addition of blue-gray, teal, yellow, dark pink, and amber should be all the categorical colors you need. If you find yourself in a situation where you need more than this, I would encourage you to think about the story you are telling with your data and whether the visual will effectively tell that story. Feel free to reach out to me (Kaila Manca) with questions and inquiries.
