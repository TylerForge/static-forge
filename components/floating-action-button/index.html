Floating action button

A floating action button (FAB) represents the primary action of a screen.

Interactive demo

Guidance

A floating action button (FAB) performs the primary, or most common, action on a screen. It appears in front of all screen content, typically as a circular shape with an icon in its center. FABs come in three types: regular, mini, and extended.

Only use a FAB if it is the most suitable way to present a screen’s primary action.

Placement

FABs can attach to top app bars and the edge of some components.

Don’t.

Individual components, such as cards, cannot each have their own FAB.

Don’t.

Don’t layer badges or other elements in front of a FAB.

Extended FAB

The extended FAB is wider, and it includes a text label. It may also include an icon if appropriate. On desktop, the extended FAB may be placed at the edge of a component or at the bottom right of the screen. On mobile, the extended FAB should be placed at the bottom right or bottom center. 

Best practices

  • Use a FAB for primary, positive actions, such as Create, Share, Start a process.
  • Avoid using FAB for minor or destructive actions such as Archive, Delete, Error, Cut text.

Don’t.

Don’t display multiple FABs on a single screen.

Don’t.

Don’t layer badges or other elements in front of a FAB.

Don’t.

Don’t use ambiguous iconography.

Don’t.

Don’t place text in a regular FAB.
