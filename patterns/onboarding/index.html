Onboarding

There are several methods for onboarding users into your product, we favor a model that is informative without being intrusive: see below for information regarding how to implement it in your application.

Guidance

Onboarding flow: Feature discovery model

An onboarding flow Kaila did for the Citizen Dashboard application. Kathy Wolf also implemented a similar design in Modria.

This model was chosen after A/B tests to determine what the least intrusive, most informative model of onboarding was. The original version (found in this Invision prototype and shown in the screenshot below) asked a series of questions to users to “customize” their dashboard experience, but it was too off-putting as the user’s initial interaction with the product. 

Onboarding flow: Information collection model

Use this pattern when the main goal of onboarding is to collect some information from users in order to customize their account experience. Instead of displaying new features, users will enter data to quickstart their app experience.

With this model, be careful not to overwhelm users with too many fields to fill in. Limit your flow to five pages or fewer and aim for nine or fewer pieces of information for users to fill in. For other examples, check out Google Material’s onboarding patterns.

Tracking onboarding in the back office

This is how Portico invites users and tracks and manages onboarding from the back-end, and overlaps a little with the roles/permissions system. You can tell who exists in the system (users), who has been invited to onboard (invites) and what roles each of these people have.

Implementation

  • The backdrop is #000000 at 70% opacity, except for the circular section over the feature you are highlighting. The size of the circle should be determined based on the size of the feature. Try to focus the user’s attention on only what’s important to take in, but do not make the circle too small to understand the content and context.
  • The arrows display in #FEC926 for sufficient contrast with the backdrop. 

  • Text should be white (#FFFFFF), medium weight, and 16px minimum, in order to provide sufficient contrast. 

  • When implemented, clicking anywhere on the screen should advance to the next screen.
